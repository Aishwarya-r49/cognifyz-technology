Sunita Williams

Pioneering NASA Astronaut & Record‑Breaking Space Explorer

Early Life & Education

Born September 19, 1965 in Euclid, Ohio, Sunita Lyn Williams graduated from the U.S. Naval Academy (B.S. Physical Science, 1987), followed by an M.S. in Engineering Management from Florida Tech in 1995.

Naval Career

  • Commissioned in 1987 as a Navy Ensign, designated naval aviator in 1989.
  • Logged over 3,000 flight hours across 30+ aircraft.
  • Supported operations such as Desert Shield and Hurricane Andrew relief.

NASA & Space Missions

  • Selected by NASA in 1998; trained in shuttle and ISS systems.
  • Served on three ISS missions (2006–2007, 2012, 2024–2025); 608 total days in space .
  • First woman to run a marathon in space (Boston Marathon on ISS treadmill, 2007).
  • Completed 9 EVAs (spacewalks) totaling 62 h 6 min—the most cumulative EVA time of any female astronaut.
  • Piloted Boeing’s first crewed Starliner flight in 2024 and returned via SpaceX's Crew‑9 in March 2025.

Awards & Legacy

Williams has been honored with the Defense Superior Service Medal, Legion of Merit, Navy Commendation/Service Medals, and is an advocate for STEM and women in science.

Her upbringing and traditions commemorate her Indian and Slovenian heritage, including carrying cultural items to space.
